# Pierre's Wine Kill List — Updated March 2026
## The "Dead Fruit" Rule

**Palate Profile**: "Necromancer" — prefers wines where primary fruit has died, revealing earth/rock/skeleton.

**Key Insight** (Mar 2026): Levet 2022 Côte-Rôtie failed because solar vintage = baby fat fruit covering the meat. Las Cases 2012 hit because 14 years = fruit is dead, only graphite/cedar remains.

---

## Vintage Rules

| Rule | Target | Avoid |
|------|--------|-------|
| Age | 10+ years (2016 or older) | Young wines with "baby fat" |
| Vintages | Cool/classic years | Solar/hot years (2018-2022) for young wines |
| Style | Skeleton (earth/rock/tar) | Lush/primary fruit |
| Regions | Cornas, Bordeaux (aged), Bandol (aged), Dunn Napa | Côte-Rôtie (too floral) |
